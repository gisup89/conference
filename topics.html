Call for Papers


Topic: Data science and analytics in new mobility systems: ridesharing, connected and autonomous vehicles, e-scooter/bike sharing, driverless demand responsive transit, and microcar.

Data science and analytics are disciplines that develop methodologies to collect, analyze, and interpret information generated in each field of society. A transportation area has also paid a lot of attention to discovering the specifics of extracted insights from mobility data. This approach typically includes the fields of data mining, prediction, machine learning, predictive analytics, statistics, and text analytics. As data grows at an alarming rate, private and public sectors are racing to harness insights from their data. Transportation practitioners need to incorporate predictive insights, forecasting, and optimization strategies into operational systems to realize the movement of people and goods.

Our road network is too complicated to solve many conventional theories or methodologies issues. In addition, new mobility modes such as ridesharing, autonomous, connected, e-scooter/bike sharing, and DRT have been added to the current transport systems. These new mobility modes are embedded with IoT devices and many sensors such as cameras, Lidars, accelerometers, IR sensors, light sensors, ultrasonic sensors, and so on. Also, infrastructures are equipped with those sensors to communicate with other infrastructure and mobility modes. This leads to data science and analytics becoming critical skills for analyzing complex mobility.

This Special Issue aims to collate state-of-the-art research that addresses challenges in the extensibility of data application in the new mobility field. Therefore, this special issue encourages researchers, practitioners, and planners to share their knowledge and skills in tackling data intelligence issues. Published articles must have clear relevance to data science and analytics. In addition to its archival mission, the journal aims to help professionals and researchers, particularly those involved in multidisciplinary projects, apply advances in data intelligence theory, methodology, and technology to their application areas. Comprehensive review papers are also very welcomed. Potential topics include but are not limited to:

■ Decision making with data-driven machine learning
■ Transport network modeling with big data for planning
■ Data visualization as a tool for providing insights from big data
■ Data fusion from multi-sensors for creating new data set to enhance the analysis
■ Innovative mobility data manipulation/imputation for better input data
■ A survey in data intelligence of transport explaining what the opinions from operators on data science and analytics are
■ A case study in new mobility to exhibit successful implementation for the new modes
■ A perception study in mobility framework as a sound guide for practitioners
